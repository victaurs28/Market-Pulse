How to Read This Report

This report uses a specific set of technical labels that describe where a stock sits relative to its own moving averages. These are not opinions - they are mechanical readings of price versus trend.

Trend Signals

Buy Zone
Price is within roughly 10% above its key moving averages and above the longer-term trend. The sweet spot for new entries. Not chasing, not catching a falling knife.
Warming Up
Price has moved 10-20% above its key averages. Momentum is strong but the stock is starting to stretch. Hold if you own it. Wait for a dip if you do not.
Extended
More than 20% above key averages. A mean-reversion pullback is increasingly likely. Do not initiate new positions here.
Pullback
Price has dipped below its shorter-term average but remains above the longer-term trend. Often creates buying opportunities within uptrends.
Downtrend
Price below its 200-day moving average. The trend is broken. Do not buy until the regime changes.

Resistance Signals

Blue Sky
Above the 40-week, 100-week, and 200-week averages. No overhead resistance. The cleanest path higher.
Light Resistance
Below the 40-week but above the 100-week and 200-week. Some overhead supply. Expect chop.
Heavy Resistance
Below the 200-week moving average. Wall of sellers above. Combined with Downtrend, this is the worst setup in the screen.

Banks Screened

The banking sector is one of the strongest technical stories in this entire market. We screened over 200 bank stocks and the picture is remarkably uniform: the vast majority are in uptrends with Blue Sky resistance. This is not a case of a few names carrying the sector. The breadth is extraordinary. Nearly every community and regional bank in the screen is in either Warming Up or Buy Zone on the weekly timeframe, with almost all of them in Blue Sky overhead.

This is happening despite the broader financials ETF (XLF) being down -6.0% YTD and in a daily downtrend. The divergence tells you something important: big bank weakness (driven by names like JPM, BAC, and the alt asset managers) is masking what is actually a very healthy underlying picture in community and regional banking. The KRE regional bank ETF, up +8.6% YTD in Buy Zone with Blue Sky, captures this reality better than XLF does.

Key insight: Do not use XLF as your banking barometer right now. KRE tells a completely different story, and the underlying breadth confirms it. Over 95% of the banks in this screen are in healthy uptrends.

The Bottom Line

What's Working

Breadth - 95%+ of screened banks in uptrends. Extremely rare.

Community banks - Strongest sub-sector. Blue Sky everywhere.

Mid-cap regionals - CFG, MTB, FITB, PNC, USB all Warming Up.

Buy Zone setups - HIFS, TFC, ZION, EWBC, FNB for clean entries.

What's Not

Big banks - JPM, BAC, WFC all pulling back on daily.

Capital One - In downtrend. Consumer credit risk.

Neobanks/Fintech - DAVE, TBBK. Fintech weakness bleeding in.

Divergence names - BANF, CBSH, FFIN. Weekly/daily disagreeing.

The banking sector is in its best technical shape since the 2023 regional bank crisis. The story is not about big banks - it is about the massive breadth of strength in community and regional names. Money is rotating down-cap within financials, and the screen is pointing clearly at where the opportunities are. Buy Zone names with Blue Sky and room to their highs are where you want to be.

When 95% of an entire sector is trending in the same direction, pay attention.
