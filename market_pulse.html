How to Read This Report

This report uses a specific set of technical labels that describe where a stock or ETF sits relative to its own moving averages. These are not opinions - they are mechanical readings of price versus trend. If you have never used moving average systems before, read this section first.

Trend Signals

Every name gets a trend reading on two timeframes - weekly and daily. These tell you whether something is in a healthy uptrend, getting stretched, pulling back, or breaking down.

Buy Zone
Price is in a healthy range near its moving averages - within roughly 10% above its key averages and above the longer-term trend. This is the sweet spot. The trend is intact, the stock has not run too far too fast, and you are not chasing. If you are going to add exposure, this is typically the best regime to do it in.
Warming Up
Price has moved 10-20% above its key averages. Momentum is strong and accelerating, but the stock is starting to stretch. Not a great place to initiate a new position, but not a sell signal either. If you already own it, hold. If you do not, consider waiting for a pullback back to Buy Zone.
Extended
Price is more than 20% above its key averages. The caution zone. The trend may still be valid, but a mean-reversion pullback is increasingly likely. Buying here means chasing, and even a healthy pullback can feel like a 10-15% drawdown. Extended does not mean "sell everything" - it means "do not add here, wait for a reset."
Pullback
Price has dipped below its shorter-term average but is still above the longer-term average. The uptrend is intact, but the stock is taking a breather. Pullbacks within uptrends are normal and often create buying opportunities. The question is whether it resolves higher or breaks lower.
Downtrend
Price has fallen below its 200-day moving average. The longer-term trend is broken. Do not buy until the regime changes. The most common mistake is trying to "buy the dip" in a genuine downtrend. A stock that looks cheap often just gets cheaper.

Resistance Signals

Every name also gets a resistance reading. When a stock falls from a high, everyone who bought near that high is underwater. When it rallies back, those people sell to "get back to even," creating resistance. The more time spent at higher prices, the harder the climb back.

Blue Sky
Price is above its 40-week, 100-week, and 200-week averages. No meaningful overhead resistance. When a stock is in Buy Zone with Blue Sky, that is the cleanest technical setup you can find.
Light Resistance
Above the 100-week and 200-week averages but below the 40-week. Some overhead supply exists but the longer-term trend is intact. Expect choppy action before a resolution.
Overhead Supply
Above the 200-week average but below the 100-week. Significant resistance above. Rallies tend to stall. The path higher will not be smooth.
Heavy Resistance
Below the 200-week moving average. Sustained rallies are rare without a fundamental catalyst that changes the narrative. Combined with a Downtrend signal, this is the worst setup in the screen - technically dead until proven otherwise.

Putting It Together

The best setup is Buy Zone + Blue Sky - healthy trend, not stretched, nothing overhead. The worst is Downtrend + Heavy Resistance - broken trend, wall of sellers above. One more metric: P/52W High is the current price as a percentage of its 52-week high. A stock at 95% is near its highs; a stock at 10% is near its lows.

The Bottom Line

This market is telling you a clear story. The assets tied to the physical world - metals, energy, nuclear, infrastructure, manufacturing, international economies - are in sustained uptrends with Blue Sky above them. The assets tied to the digital world - software, crypto, payments, US mega-cap growth - are in persistent downtrends with heavy resistance overhead.

For the technically-minded, the message is: own what's working, avoid what's not, and be patient with the extended names. The best entries come on pullbacks into Buy Zones within strong trends - not by chasing. The worst risk is catching a falling knife in a broken sector, no matter how cheap the fundamentals look.

Follow the money. Right now, it's flowing into things you can touch.
